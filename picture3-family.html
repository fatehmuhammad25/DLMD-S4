Sentence Levels in English

In English grammar, sentences can be classified into several levels. Here are the explanations:

1. Non-Clause

A group of words without a subject and a verb.

Example: Playing in the school yard.

2. Simple Sentence

A sentence with one subject and one verb.

Example: Students study English.

3. Compound Sentence

Two simple sentences joined by a coordinating conjunction (and, but, so, etc.).

Example: Students study, and teachers teach.

4. Complex Sentence

One independent clause combined with one or more dependent clauses using subordinating conjunctions (because, although, if, etc.).

Example: Students study because exams are coming.

5. Compound-Complex Sentence

A combination of at least two independent clauses and one or more dependent clauses.

Example: Students study, and teachers teach because they want good results.

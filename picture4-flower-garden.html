Sentence Levels in English

In English grammar, sentences can be classified into several levels based on their structure:

1. Non-Clause

A group of words without a subject and a verb.

Example: Watering the flowers in the morning.

2. Simple Sentence

A sentence with one subject and one verb.

Example: The flowers bloom.

3. Compound Sentence

Two simple sentences joined by a coordinating conjunction (and, but, so, etc.).

Example: The flowers bloom, and the bees fly around.

4. Complex Sentence

One independent clause combined with one or more dependent clauses.

Example: The flowers bloom because the weather is good.

5. Compound-Complex Sentence

A combination of at least two independent clauses and one or more dependent clauses.

Example: The flowers bloom, and the bees fly around because the sun is shining.
